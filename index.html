AMBEDKAR: A Multi-level Bias Elimination through a Decoding Approach with Knowledge Augmentation for Robust Constitutional Alignment.

S. Mukhopadhyay, A. Kasat, S. Dubey, R. Karthikeyan, et al.

TL;DR : Large Language Models exhibit strong caste- and religion-based biases in Indian contexts that existing, Western-centric mitigation methods fail to address. We propose AMBEDKAR, an inference-time bias mitigation framework inspired by the Indian Constitution, which introduces a Constitution-Aware Decoding Layer without retraining or parameter updates. By reinterpreting speculative decoding as a fairness mechanism—where a small model generates and a constitutionally guided large model verifies—AMBEDKAR achieves up to 26.41% absolute bias reduction while remaining computationally efficient.

Bias Meters across Religions and Castes: Our benchmark dataset has been meticulously curated to represent 6 major religions and 136 caste groups, providing a comprehensive resource for stress-testing language models in the Indian sociocultural context.
Overview of the AMBEDKAR framework: A draft language model generates speculative hypotheses, which are evaluated by a verifier model under both original and counterfactual contexts. Candidate completions are scored using distributional divergence metrics, and the token with the highest cross-context consistency and contextual stability is selected for generation.

BioRiskBench: An Adversarial Benchmark for Dual Use Risk of Biological eapons

TL;DR: Scaling frontier language models embeds dual use biological knowledge that remains accessible despite high refusal rates, showing that capability and biosecurity compliance are not orthogonal. Using BioRiskBench-η and a novel cognitive-drift jailbreak, we demonstrate that current alignment methods provide only brittle, surface-level defenses, with models retaining actionable wet-lab knowledge that can be reconstructed under adversarial pressure. We introduce new risk metrics to quantify this gap and argue that mitigating biological risk requires moving beyond post hoc filtering toward intrinsic latent unlearning.
